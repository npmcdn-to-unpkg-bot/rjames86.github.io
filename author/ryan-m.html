Gear Setup

I’ve gotten a few questions about the bags and gear that I’m using so I figured it’s worth taking a few pictures and listing off what I’m going to be using during the ride.

Bike

I was riding the Volagi Viaje XL (steel) for the last year. In March 2016, I decided to upgrade to the Volagi Viaje Ti and do a custom build. The component set is Ultregra Di2, which I’ve absolutely loved. Volagi Ignite VL wheels with a SON28 dynamo hub and Luxos U light.

Bags

  1. Ortlieb Ultimate 6

    • I’ve never settled on the perfect handlebar bag, but in terms of size and fit, I’ve been really happy with this bag. It’s not too big, but just big enough to fit my sleeping bag with a little room to spare for leg and arm warmers.

  2. Revelate Tangle Frame Bag

    • One of my newest additions. So far so good. The bag doesn’t rub at all against my legs while riding. The Viaje frame has an unusual triangle, but it fits just fine.

  3. Timbuk2 Goody Box

    • I’ve had this thing for a long time. Great for keeping keys and some snacks readily available.

  4. Revelate Jerrycan

    • This thing doesn’t hold much, but it’s just big enough to fit 2 tubes and a few small extras.

  5. Revelate Pika

    • I love this bag. It can hold a lot (6-12L), but also packs down small enough where I can just keep my tools and tubes on shorter rides.

Camping Gear

  1. REI Flash Bag + compression sack
    • I bought this bag specifically for the tour. I have a bag already, but it didn’t pack down nearly to this size.
  2. Big Agnes tent
    • I’ve had this thing for a long time as well. I’ve removed the tent poles and put them into the frame bag so that it’ll pack down small enough to easily fit in the seat bag.

  3. REI Flash sleeping pad

    • Another new addition. Haven’t used it yet so no real opinions there. I bought this one mostly because of the weight and size.

  4. ShowerPass rain pants

    • Just in case…

  5. Cocoon Travel Pillow

    • It’s not fun sleeping without a pillow. Some say just stuff all your extra stuff in a bag and sleep on that, but I want a “real” pillow. It packs down small enough where it really isn’t taking up too much precious space

  6. ShowerPass Elite rain jacket

    • I love this jacket. It’s expensive, but it’s kept me warm and dry on some pretty nasty rides. It vents well and also keeps out the wind well enough that I’ll just wear it on colder days to stay warm.

I’ve gotten a lot of questions about how I did the third water bottle cage. The one advantage to the Ti frame over the steel Viaje is that it already has screw mounts for the third cage. Nothing fancy here. Just screwed it in and done!

I get a fair amount of grief for riding on Compass tires. They don’t have nearly the puncture resistance of something like Gatorskins, but they’re so comfortable that I can’t switch. I have a fresh pair ready to go for the ride and will be carrying a spare.

Missoula to San Francisco: The Route

My plan is to depart on September 7 from Missoula, MT. I’ll be following the TransAmerica route all the way to the Oregon coast where I’ll meet up with the Pacific Coast Trail and ride south all the way to San Francisco.

My hope is that I can average roughly 100 miles a day for the entire trip, putting me at around 14 total days if I don’t stop.

Getting ready for the ride

I had some free time over the weekend to see how much gear I could get on my bike. It was a good chance to see what I already had and what I still needed to buy for the trip. I already had the seat bag, and the frame bag came in the mail today.

Everything went together nicely. The tent poles fit perfectly in the frame bag. The bag even has velcro straps specifically to hold things like this. The tent fits in the seat bag with plenty of room for other things. The sleeping bag fits up on the handlebars with plenty of room to spare, but I didn’t like how I had it strapped on. I’ll be ordering the Revelate Harness to hold it on there. You can see in the picture that the bag is pushing down on my light (which I’ve since tightened up so that shouldn’t happen).

I have a small bag that’ll sit on the top tube against the stem and I’m also ordering the Revelate Jerrycan that’ll also go on the top tube against the seat post.

It’s also nice that the frame bag has a small opening in the front to allow cabling to be run through it. I have the USB cable from the Luxos U running into the bag so I can charge my phone or extra battery packs while I’m riding. I’ve also moved one of my water bottle cages to under the frame since it didn’t fit well in the triangle while the frame bag was attached.
